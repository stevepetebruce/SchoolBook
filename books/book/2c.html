They flew slowly over to the battle and the people could hear the roar of their engines as they approached. They had never seen anything like this before and they could not have known what damage the giant bombs in these planes could do. The planes drew closer and closer to their target city, Rotterdam, which was many kilometers from where the Frank family lived, but other families scared by these new weapons ran inside to hide from their attack. The planes were now over their target city. The pilots released the bombs.

Thousands of bombs were dropped and destroyed the heart of the Rotterdam. The bombs and the fires they caused destroyed tens of thousands of homes, making many people homeless. This was too much. The people surrendered to Hitler's army and Otto, Edith, Margot and Anne, like everyone else, worried what would happen to them now that Hitler was in control of their home.

At first, after the fighting stopped, nothing much changed. It was just quiet.
