You might be wondering how we know so much about the Frank family as their story is so secret? Anne kept a diary. Almost every day she would write down what had happened in the secret house or in the outside world that she would hear on the news. She would tell the diary her feelings, her thoughts and what was worrying her. The diary was like a close friend who was always ready to listen but only spoke back what she had already said. That is a good friend to have when you are forced to live in silence, squashed in with seven other people...

Anne wrote about her dream of becoming a journalist: she knew that she had to keep up with her schoolwork to stay smart and get ahead in life, even though she, nor any of the other children in the secret house, actually went to school anymore. She knew she wanted to do more than her mother and the other women, who she thought just went about their housework and were then forgotten. Anne had ambitions and dreams and she knew she had to study hard to make them come true.
