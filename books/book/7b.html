Otto Frank, who remained in Camp A, was the only member of the Frank family to survive long enough to see The Alliance liberation. He returned to the city where his family's last home was, the secret house, and went about trying to find his family. He learned that Edith, his wife had died and then later that his two daughters, Anne and Margot had also died in the camps. Over 100,000 Jews, men and women, boys and girls were forced to the camps. And only 5,000 had survived. Otto was one of those 5,000.

He knew that people had to learn the truth of what had happened to the Jews when Hitler's men invaded their home. The horrible way they were treated, not being allowed to drive a car, or go to the cinema. And how they had to hide for years in secret to save themselves. And being locked up in the camps. And the killings and the other horrors.
